If you've been in your career for just about any length of time, you might've come across something called The Dunning-Kruger Effect. If you haven't heard of it, Wikipedia has a [great synopsis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunning%E2%80%93Kruger_effect) on it. It's the hypothesis that "people with low ability at a task overestimate their ability [to do the task]". As a chart, here's how that looks:

![](/images/dunning-kruger-chart.png)

Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal popularized a term called "Mount Stupid", which relates the "willingness to opine on a topic" vs the "knowledge of that topic".
